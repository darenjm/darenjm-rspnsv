Fear

January 29, 2017  |  Daren Morreale

This weeks post will be a mostly unedited, stream of conceiousness session I had while riding the bus over two days last week. Here it is.

Fear is fuel. Anxiety is the exhaust of burning that fuel. If you use fear to move or drive you forward you're staying ahead of the exhaust. If you allow fear to paralyse you you will choke on the fumes. Embrace fear, let it be the fuel for your engine and keep moving forward.

I learned today from Jocko that one of the ways he is able to lead in stressful and dangerous times, even just function, is he detaches. Usually detaching leads to bad outcomes in everyday life, marriage in particular. But in this case I get it. If you can step back, take a birds eye view of yourself in the moment you can see things. You get perspective that you can't get up close. That is the approach I am going to take during this time of uncertainty and fear. I will stay ahead of the fumes of anxiety. I will detach from the moment and I will guide myself as if I am talking to a friend. I will see my situation from afar and look around and see things I just can't see from the the inside. About

I am putting this site together to keep track of my progress as a web developer. So far I have taught myself HTML, CSS a bit of PHP (enough to convert my static websites to WordPress ready dynamic sites) and SEO best practices. The next step is JavaScript, but as of this writing, I’ve decided to just move ahead and use jQuery plug-ins when and where I need it. Don’t want to get bogged down because I know the opportunity to learn JavaScript will come as I move along.

As I progress I will be building, re-building, revising and updating this site as I build my skills. Everything will be designed and coded by me as a way to show my clients what I am capable of doing and how I can help them in their businesses.

I do have a background in graphic design (20 years). I worked in print design, from big ad agencies in NYC to small design shops in San Francisco and Seattle, and I spent 2 and a half years building PowerPoint presentations, primarily for Microsoft ... a lot of Photoshop!

I have always loved building things and I spent 5 years as a professional carpenter and a custom cabinet maker in Seattle. In this role I helped to remodel homes and build exhibits for museums in the Puget Sound area. Although I no longer do this professionally, I continue to build. And now, I build websites. The challenges in this line of work are addictive.

The obstacle is the way is a philosophy (Stoicism) that works for me and drives me forward, and this is how I measure my progress. I see each obstacle as a learning opportunity. I celebrate my successes (briefly) and I analyse my failures (quickly) then move on.

I also keep a journal. I dump the accumulating contents of my brain there on a nearly daily basis. Sometimes I wake up and the first thing I do is reach for my book and start writing. At times it feels like I’m emptying the trash. It’s also where I give myself a kick in the ass when I need it. I am now continuing it here, in the form of a blog so check that out too. There I write what ever is on my mind that particular day, but mostly I will stick to web stuff.
